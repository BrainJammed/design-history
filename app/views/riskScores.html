    Iteration 1

    In iteration 1 we had an ideation session (Martin Liddament , Simon Scott, Tim Brazier. Konstantinos Bougioukntolaptsis). We brought different ideas from other areas and sketched our own ones based on the user needs.

    The POVs we used for ideation where:

    • As a probation officer I want to see every risk scores in one place because I don't want to click through different screens.
    • As a probation officer I want to understand the progress of someone's risk because I need to understnad what has hapened and assess them properly.
    • As a probation officer I want to see information about each actuarial score beause I need to refresh my memory of what each one is looking at and what it means.
    Figma link to ideation page

    The three ideas above were selected after dot-voting and used for the design of the first iteration that we tested with our participants.

    In the first page we see the dashboard where the individual's risk scores are displayed all in one place. The arrows next to the scores indicate the trajectory of the score. A timeline under the scores shows the assessment history of the individual. When we click to a score we deep dive into details about it.

    In page 2 we see the OGRS3 detailed view. There is a score line with the current score displayed there. At the top of the page there is information about what this score is about. At the bottom of the page we see a timeline specifically for that score and a trajectory of it.

    In page 3 we see the detailed view of ROSH. ROSH does not have a score or a scoreline but has a table showing the individual's risk for each category. Again there is information about the score at the top and a timeline at the bottom

    Testing prototype 1

    During testing we tried to understand:

    • how officers are currently using risk scores to do an assessment.
    • the mental model they have for each risk score and which one they believe is the most useful. They were asked to put them in an order of importance
    • how much they are usigng scores and their personal experience to decide on the assessment
    • what they think this prototype shows
    • if the prototype would help them with assessments

    Positives

    Things that tested well and we bring in the next iteration:

    • Colours have worked really well. Their meaning was well understood and drawed attention. Was easy to have a first pass on the user's profile and understand what is going on.
    • The scores timelines tested well also. It was good for users to see where exactly the scores sits withing the range of the timeline.

    Negatives

    Things that didn't test well or were not understood and we will change in the next iteration:

    • Arrows were not understood so well. Users thought it was a way to expand the risk scores.
    • There were missing risk scores. Participants said they wanted to see all risk scores on the dashboard.
    • The risk scores names font colour seemed faded and hard to read
    • The risk scores guidelines were taking a significant part of the screen and we wanted users to focus their attention more on the score line
    • The ROSH risk table was not easy to read and the arrows were misleading.

    General feel

    Iteration 2

    In iteration 2 we brought the feedback from the session to analysis to understand how participants feel about risk scores, how they are currently using them for assessments, what their painpoints are, what do they make of the prototype and what we need to change for the next round of testing.

    Figma link to making changes to the prototype after the first round of testing

    During testing we tried to understand:

    • the impact of changes to the prototype.
    • how much they are usigng scores and their personal experience to decide on the assessment
    • how effective were the changes in terms of helping participants make assessment decisions

    Positives

    Things that tested well and we bring in the next iteration:

    • Arrows have been understood better.
    • Colours are helping participants understand the risk levels better.
    • The risk scores guidelines tested well also. Participants cliked on the reveal link and mentioned it was great to have that information there as new officers need to have that information available so they can understand what each risk score means.

    Negatives

    Things that didn't test well or were not understood and we will change in the next iteration:

    • The risk scores order needed to change. Participants said that they feel that ROSH is the most significant one when assessing risk and RSR is a new risk score that they do not feel is accurate
    • The risk scores lines needed clarification as per the border values as risk scores use different values.
    • The content in the drill down pages for risk scores had to signify we are talking about the same individual we were assessing on the dashboard.

    General feel

    Iteration 3

    In iteration 3 the changes included bringing the ROSH information into the dashboard as per what the participants said. They need to have that information ready there because it helps them with their assessment more than other risk scores. The risk scores timeline was designed in such a way that now shows the border values for each risk score. Finally we had some content changes in the drill down page.

    Figma link to making changes to the prototype after the first round of testing

    Positives

    Things that tested well and we bring in the next iteration:

    • Participants liked the fact the the ROSH information was on the dashboard.
    • Percentage reminders at the borders of timelines were helpful.

    Negatives

    Things that didn't test well or were not understood and we will change in the next iteration:

    • Arrows need more work.
    • The risk scores lines needed clarification as per the border values as risk scores use different values.

    General feel

    Next steps

    Next steps is to pass our findings and designs to other teams for testing on their products and get their results. We need to ensure we are consisnte across service and that each product displays risk scores in a away that is useful for each side of the product. Allocation and workforce management teams has taken oer and started testing a view of our design to their product. We are expecting their findings soon.
